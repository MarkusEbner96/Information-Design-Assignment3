Section 1 of 3 · The slow speed
Vienna's car fleet: still mostly fossil

Since 2011, the total fleet composition has changed — but slowly. Petrol and diesel still dominate. Electric vehicles are growing, but from a tiny base.

Why so slow?
Cars have long lifespans (15–20 yrs). Even rapid new-registration growth takes decades to replace the existing stock. This is the "fleet inertia" problem.

Section 2 of 3 · The fast speed
New registrations: a dramatic shift since 2020

Factory-new vehicles entering the fleet tell a very different story. The share of electric and hybrid among new registrations has surged — this is where policy incentives are most visible.

2020 turning point
Federal subsidies + COVID recovery packages triggered a sharp jump in EV new registrations.
Company cars dominate
Most new EVs are registered by companies, not private households — skewing the headline figures.

Section 3 of 3 · The social speed
Used cars: when does the transition reach everyone?

Most private households buy used cars, not new ones. The used-car market is therefore the key indicator of when the e-mobility transition becomes truly accessible — beyond companies and high-income buyers.

The lag effect
A car registered new in 2021 typically enters the used market 3–5 years later. So the used-EV boom is still coming — and this chart shows its early signals.

Interaction inventory
Hover tooltips — all three charts show exact values on hover (year, category, count/share). Present on all three data views.
View toggle (Section 1) — "Absolute numbers" vs "Share (%)" on the fleet stock chart. Triggers chart re-render.
Year range slider (Section 1) — filters the fleet stock chart to a custom time window. Live update.
Drive-type filter (Section 2) — filter line chart by BEV / Hybrid / Fossil / All. Multi-select buttons.
Annotation callouts — key insight boxes (2020 turning point, EU 2035, "access gap") are static — always visible, part of the editorial narrative.
Next / Back navigation — footer buttons advance the linear story. Top nav allows non-linear jumping. Progress bar reflects current position.
